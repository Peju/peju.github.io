MVP AR App (site under construction)

Summary:

Using modern technology such as Augmented Reality (AR) to help future MassMutual interns and new hires to navigate the Springfield campus with interactive features in a fun and effective manner. This would be an internal app and could only be downloaded and accessed within the company’s campus internet.

Features: Using these scannable stickers placed throughout the campus, it would pop up and provide a 3D cube where each side has ways of consuming information regarding the department they’re in. Sides of the cubes would have an introduction video of a member of the department sharing what they do. Basic text info regarding their department. A map of where they’re located on campus, etc. This is an interactive way of learning and an element that could be gamified through the sticker collection feature.

Tools: Unity, Axure, MS Powerpoint, Illustrator Members: 5 (My role: Product Lead/PM)

Process: Pain points: New employees/interns have no idea have to navigate the large and vast MM Springfield Campus. They also don’t know which departments do what and what their responsibilities are. This lack of understanding could slow down work progress if someone needs assistance from another unit, but don’t know who or where to contact them. These problems could be considered process pain point.

Customer Discovery: Besides the member’s experience of having issues navigating the campus and figuring out which building is which. What section of the room is where? Asking random MM employees where is X place? We wanted to provide new employees with a smoother way to onboard and learn their new setting of the campus.

We asked with qualitative data through basic testimonies from our fellow interns and got basic quantitative data using a 1-5 Likert Scale (1 = extremely difficult, 5 = very easy) how easy was it to navigate the campus and find out where certain buildings or conference rooms where. Our basic customer discovery data showed that the current navigation features such as the internal map the company provides wasn’t helpful enough and not visually too useful.

KPI: Because this project was to produce a MVP of our product, we didn’t get to fully measure KPIs. However, some KPIs to consider if this product was to be worked in the future would be the following.

Downloads: The amount of MM employees who have downloaded the app. Quality of Session: Is the user using the app for short periods of time (the app wasn’t useful) or they’re using it for too long (the app has them confused and doesn’t effectively solve their problems). There would need to be a discussion regarding what’s considered a good duration for a session. Navigation Timer: Once the desired location is set, was the estimated timer accurate in assisting navigation for the person (user). Video Views: Are people watching these videos that are supposed to help with providing a new understanding of what the department does? Stickers Activated: Are people using the sticker feature to learn about the department and have fun?
